# FeatureProject

Developed with Unreal Engine 5



This is a rough recreation of the Ocarina Playing mechanic from the aptly named *Legend of Zelda: Ocarina of Time*.



There are UI elements demonstrating how to use the mechanic inside the game, but just in case more explanation is needed:



Pressing "P" changes between the normal menu and one of two "Playing" menus. The normal menu is when you can move around but can't play.
Playing Menus let you play notes, but not move the character *OR* the camera. If the player is in a "Learning Zone," which is an area directly in front of a stone displaying the notes of the song, they will open a Learning Menu. Otherwise, the regular Playing Menu is opened.

While in a playing menu, the Arrow Keys and Left Shift will all play a note and add it to an order of notes. If you play a song that you get from a Learning Zone, the player will be warped to it. You can only play songs you have learned, as playing songs without having learned them will result in nothing.







I would also like to use this space to credit the construction and inspiration for the music and sounds played throughout:



The songs are brief snippets of movements from the song "Elements," composed by Brian Balmages. This is a song I played in Highschool Band that I loved a lot and realized I could use as my songs for the game.

The software I used to produce the songs and noises was "Beepbox," a program I found sometime in Middle/Highschool. It's a fun and simple website that I used to compose rough imitations of the movements from "Elements."
